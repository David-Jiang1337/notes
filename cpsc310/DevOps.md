In the earliest periods of software development, development and technology operations were delegated to separate teams of engineers, each with a distinct role in the software. Developers were responsible for writing the code, fixing bugs, and ensuring correctness. Technology operations would then be responsible for ensuring that the software could be practically deployed, determining system requirements and platform compatibility. DevOps is the practice of unifying these two previously separated responsibilities, shared among a single team.

DevOps sought to address challenges that arose from separating development from technical operations. Namely, separate teams led to slower and less constructive communication, which ultimately increased costs and deployment times, and heightened the likelihood of failure.
